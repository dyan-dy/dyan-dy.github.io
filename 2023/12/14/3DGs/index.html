How to represent Geometry? - 3DGs
更新于 2023-12-15

Rasterization

The process of finding all the pixels in an image that are occupied by a geometric primitive is called rasterization.

—— Fundamental of computer graphics, fourth edition, written by Steve Marschner, Peter Shirley, et al.

The main goal of rasterization is to project the 3D triangle into the project plane and pixelization it. A normal way of rasterization is using triangles. In this way the problem comes to, “is the pixel inside the triangle?”

Triangle is the most basic polygon and it owns special property of planar-garanteed. In order to make sure our pixel is always in the triangle, we just need to use cross product to determine whether a point is located inside a triangle by representing the pixel as the barycenter of the triangle.

Check image 3 above, we can easily know that,

$$ (AB \times AP) \cdot (BC \times BP) > 0 $$
$$ (AB \times AP) \cdot (CA \times CP) > 0$$
$$ (CA \times CP) \cdot (BC \times BP) > 0$$

, means that the pixel is inside the triangle.

Image 1 and 2 are form GAMES101: 现代计算机图形学入门; image 3 is from 知乎专栏.

Explicit/Implicit Geometry

In the explicit geometry, the shape can be directly described from vertex coordinates or equation of the boundary curve. While in implicit geometry, the shape is represented by implicit function(e.g., $x^2+y^2=r^2$) without giving information about all point-coordinates on the circle.

3D Gaussian

The shape determined by a three-dimensional Gaussian distribution is an ellipsoid. This ellipsoid is symmetric along the x, y, and z axes, and the cross-sections cut from the perpendicular plane of these symmetric axes are ellipses (or circles).

In the standard form of 3D Gaussians,

$$ G_s(x) = (\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}^3 det(\Sigma)}e^{-\frac{1}{2}(x-\mu)^T\Sigma^{-1}(x-\mu)}) $$

, $x=[a,b,c]^T$ stands for 3D vector coordinates, $\mu$ stands for the center of the ellipsoid, the covariance matrix $\Sigma=\begin{bmatrix}\sigma_{a}^2 & Cov(a,b) & Cov(a,c) \ Cov(b,a) & \sigma_{b}^2 & Cov(b,c) \ Cov(c,a) & Cov(c,b) & \sigma_{c}^{2}\end{bmatrix}$ controls the rotation and scale of the ellipsoid through 3 axes.

Splatting

As we mentioned before, the principal goal traditional rasterization is to project the 3D triangle into the plane and pixelize it. However, the pipeline in modern GPU normally is already encapsulated, and we care more about what happens before and after rasterization.

Splatting means project the 3D ellipsoid into 2D. The wording here is very vivid because normally we use “splatting” like throwing a snowball onto the wall, or the water drop dripping on the ground.

This study note is structured based on 知乎专栏.For more reading, there is a github repository for understanding the 3D Gaussian Splatting.
